Explore the Essentials

Understand what makes a great bed from the ground up

Mattress

Mattresses come in various types to suit different sleeping preferences, including memory foam, hybrid (a combination of foam and innerspring), and solid innerspring models. Each type offers unique benefits, from pressure relief to enhanced airflow and support. For increased longevity, many mattresses are designed to be double-sided, allowing you to flip them regularly. This helps maintain even wear over time, preventing sagging and extending the mattress’s overall lifespan for a better long-term investment in your sleep quality.

Bed Frames

Bed frames provide the essential support and foundation for your mattress, impacting both comfort and durability. When selecting a bed frame, it’s important to consider the spacing of the slats — they should be less than 3 inches apart to properly support the mattress and prevent sagging. If you’re using a bunkie board, this spacing can be a bit more flexible since the board offers extra support. Choosing the right bed frame with proper slat spacing helps extend the life of your mattress and ensures a stable, comfortable sleeping surface.

Sheets

Sheets are an essential part of your bedding, providing comfort and style while protecting your mattress. The quality of sheets is often measured by thread count, which is the number of threads woven into one square inch of fabric. The ideal thread count typically ranges from 200 to 400, offering a perfect balance of softness, breathability, and durability. Sheets with lower thread counts may feel rough, while very high thread counts can sometimes reduce breathability and feel heavier. Choosing sheets within this range ensures a comfortable and restful sleep experience.

Bunkie Boards

A bunkie board is a thin, sturdy platform typically made of wood or metal designed to provide solid support for mattresses, especially on low-profile bed frames like platform beds or bunk beds. Unlike traditional box springs, bunkie boards offer a slim, flat surface that helps distribute weight evenly and prevent mattress sagging, making them ideal for smaller spaces or beds with minimal clearance. They are commonly used to extend the life of a mattress and improve overall sleeping comfort.
